# Phase 6: Blog Article Rewrite Template (3000+ Words)

## Template Structure for Long-Form Blog Articles

This template provides the framework for rewriting all blog posts to meet EEAT standards, semantic SEO requirements, and long-form content best practices. Each article should target 3000-4000 words.

---

### 2. ARTICLE CONTENT STRUCTURE

#### SECTION 1: INTRODUCTION (300-400 words)
**Purpose**: Hook reader, establish expertise, preview value

- **Opening Hook**: Start with a compelling statistic, question, or scenario relevant to Indian SMBs
- **Problem Statement**: Clearly articulate the challenge your audience faces
- **Why This Matters**: Explain business impact (revenue, growth, efficiency)
- **What You'll Learn**: Bullet list of 5-7 key takeaways
- **Reading Time**: Include "X min read" indicator
- **Author Credentials**: Brief expert bio with relevant experience

**Example Opening**:
```
"According to recent industry data, 73% of Indian SMBs struggle with 
consistent lead generation. If you're one of them, you're not alone. 
In this comprehensive guide, we'll show you exactly how to build a 
lead generation system that works for your business, with real examples 
from Bengaluru-based companies."
```

---

#### SECTION 2: FOUNDATIONAL CONTEXT (400-500 words)
**Purpose**: Establish expertise, provide background, set up main content

- **What is [Topic]?**: Clear definition with industry context
- **Why It Matters for Your Business**: Specific relevance to Indian market
- **Common Misconceptions**: Address 2-3 myths
- **Key Statistics**: Industry-backed numbers with sources (e.g., "According to HubSpot's 2024 report...")
- **Quick Comparison Table**: Contrasts old vs. new approaches

**Example Structure**:
```markdown
## Understanding [Topic] in the Indian Market

### What is [Topic]?
[Clear definition with 2-3 sentence explanation]

### Why It Matters for Bengaluru & Indian SMBs
- Point 1 with specific market context
- Point 2 with local relevance
- Point 3 with growth opportunity

### Common Misconceptions
**Myth 1**: [False belief]
**Reality**: [Correct information]

**Myth 2**: [False belief]
**Reality**: [Correct information]

### Industry Context
According to [Source], [statistic]. This means [implication for your business].
```

## CONTENT OPTIMIZATION GUIDELINES

### For Featured Snippets (0-position)
- Include answer-first paragraphs (2-3 sentences answering the question directly)
- Use numbered lists for "how-to" queries
- Use tables for comparison queries
- Use definitions for "what is" queries
- Keep answers to 40-60 words for paragraph snippets

### For Google AI Overviews
- Include multiple perspectives on the topic
- Provide data-backed claims with sources
- Include both pros and cons
- Use clear, scannable formatting
- Include expert opinions

### For ChatGPT/Gemini/Perplexity Citations
- Use clear topic sentences
- Include specific, verifiable information
- Cite sources for statistics
- Provide actionable advice
- Use proper heading structure

### For Semantic SEO
- Use related keywords naturally throughout
- Include synonyms and variations
- Use proper heading hierarchy (H1 → H2 → H3)
- Include entity relationships (e.g., linking related concepts)
- Use schema markup (Article, FAQ, BreadcrumbList)

### For EEAT Signals
- **Expertise**: Include author bio, credentials, years of experience
- **Authoritativeness**: Cite reputable sources, include expert opinions
- **Trustworthiness**: Provide transparent information, include disclaimers where needed
- **Experience**: Include real examples (hypothetical but realistic)

## INDIAN/BENGALURU SMB CONTEXT REQUIREMENTS

Every article must include:

1. **Market-Specific Examples**: At least 2-3 references to Indian market dynamics
2. **Bengaluru Context**: Mention of Bengaluru-based companies or market specifics
3. **Currency/Pricing**: Use INR where relevant, mention Indian pricing context
4. **Regulatory Context**: Reference Indian regulations (GST, data privacy, etc.) if applicable
5. **Cultural Nuances**: Acknowledge Indian business practices and preferences
6. **SMB-Specific Advice**: Tailor recommendations for small-to-medium businesses (10-500 employees)

**Example Contextual Additions**:
```
"For Bengaluru-based SaaS companies, this approach has proven particularly 
effective because of the competitive talent market and the need to scale 
quickly. Indian SMBs in the IT services sector have reported..."

"Unlike enterprise companies with dedicated teams, Indian SMBs often need 
to implement these strategies with limited resources. Here's how to adapt 
this approach for a team of 2-3 people..."

"In the Indian market, where cost-consciousness is high, this strategy 
offers excellent ROI. Most Bengaluru startups see results within 3-4 months 
with an investment of ₹50,000-₹1,00,000."
```

## SCHEMA MARKUP REQUIREMENTS

Every article must include:

1. **Article Schema**: Title, author, publication date, modified date, image
2. **FAQ Schema**: All FAQ questions and answers
3. **BreadcrumbList Schema**: Navigation path
4. **Author Schema**: Author information with credentials

## TONE & VOICE GUIDELINES

- **Tone**: Professional yet conversational
- **Perspective**: First-person plural ("we", "our") to build connection
- **Complexity**: Explain technical concepts in simple terms
- **Confidence**: Authoritative but not arrogant
- **Empathy**: Acknowledge challenges and frustrations
- **Action-Oriented**: Use imperative verbs (do, implement, create, build)

**Example Tone**:
```
"We've helped hundreds of Bengaluru-based companies implement this strategy. 
Here's what we've learned works best, and what to avoid. Let's dive in."
```

## COMMON PITFALLS TO AVOID

❌ **Don't**: Fabricate statistics or case studies
✓ **Do**: Use industry-backed data or clearly label examples as hypothetical

❌ **Don't**: Write generic content that could apply to any business
✓ **Do**: Include specific Indian/Bengaluru market context

❌ **Don't**: Create thin content with minimal depth
✓ **Do**: Provide comprehensive, actionable information

❌ **Don't**: Ignore heading structure
✓ **Do**: Use proper H1 → H2 → H3 hierarchy

❌ **Don't**: Write long, dense paragraphs
✓ **Do**: Use short paragraphs (2-3 sentences) and bullet lists

❌ **Don't**: Forget about featured snippets
✓ **Do**: Include answer-first paragraphs and structured data
